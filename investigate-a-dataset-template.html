Introduction¶

The TMDB movie dataset was selected for investigating given the author's interest in The film industry. There are some factors influencing the success or failure of a movie. In this project the author analyzes the dataset to find some successful features for movies answering the questions below:

  • Which companies invested the most in film production from 2000 to 2015?
  • Which movie genres were most produced from 2000 to 2015?
  • What were the genres of the high revenue movies from 2000 to 2015?

def plot_pie_chart(sizes, labels, title):
    fig1, ax1 = plt.subplots()
    ax1.pie(sizes, labels=labels, autopct='%1.1f%%',shadow=True, startangle=90)
    ax1.axis('equal')  # Equal aspect ratio ensures that pie is drawn as a circle.
    plt.title(title)
    plt.show()

Conclusions¶

The questions proposed for this analysis were created following a logical thinking, aiming to try to connect the key points, in the author's view, of high-profit films. First of all, we identified which production companies invested the most in the period. Then we identified which genres of films were most produced year by year of the period. And finally, we analyzed the genres of the high revenue movies in the period. The point was to see if the companies that invest the most try to make high profits by producing genre films that other companies are betting on. However, most of the films produced in the period are Dramas and the companies that invested the most were successful with other genres. With the analysis, it is possible to identify that some companies, such as Paramount Pictures and Walt Disney Pictures, appear as the biggest investors in film production and some of their films were profitable success in the period. The Walt Disney Pictures is one of the companies that invests most in film production and has 25% of the most lucrative films of the period. WingNut Films has about 19% of the most lucrative films of the period and does not even appear on the list of top investors, but the films are of the genre adventure, which corresponds to 62.5% of the most lucrative genres. Thus, it can be concluded that the genre of a movie is strongly correlated with success / profit.

Limitations¶

The results are limited to the modified data set, considering many entries in the dataset have been removed due to missing data. Part of the research is based on the genre column that had an array as its value. The array was separated into columns and the genre that was found in the first position was used, considering that it was the main genre. The same was done for the production_companies column. This types of normalization can distort the result. Therefore, the results cannot be generalized, it is important to make it clear that the results should be observed as indicators.
